Why Infant Vaccines Matter

  • Protecting against serious diseases: Vaccines help protect infants from life-threatening diseases like polio, measles, and whooping cough.
  • Early protection: Vaccinating infants at the right time ensures early immunity before they are exposed to these diseases.
  • Herd immunity: Vaccinating infants helps protect others in the community, especially those who cannot be vaccinated, by creating herd immunity.
  • Long-term health: Vaccination provides long-term protection, reducing the risk of illnesses throughout childhood and adulthood.
  • Safe and effective: Infant vaccines are tested for safety and effectiveness before being approved by health authorities, making them one of the safest preventive measures available.
  • Reducing healthcare costs: Vaccination can reduce healthcare costs by preventing hospitalizations and the long-term effects of preventable diseases.
